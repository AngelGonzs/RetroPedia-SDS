The Legend of Zelda (1986)

{% include "nav_bar.html" %}

The Legend of Zelda is an action-adventure game developed and published by Nintendo for the NES console in 1986. It was designed by Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka, and it follows the adventure of a young boy named Link as he tries to rescue Princess Zelda and defeat the evil Ganon.

The game was one of the first to feature a battery-backed save system, allowing players to save their progress and continue their game later. It was also one of the first to use a non-linear gameplay style, with players being able to explore the game's world in any order they choose.

The Legend of Zelda has become one of Nintendo's most iconic franchises, with numerous sequels and spin-off titles released over the years. The series has also been adapted into a television show, comic book series, and a number of other media.
